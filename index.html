Virtual ESL Journey to Oxford

Welcome to my travel blog! Here, I'm taking you along on my trip to England. In this section, you will learn all about my experiences, language exercises, and the progress I'm making during this journey. You can follow me on my language journey to Oxford.

We invite you to a virtual language journey to England. You will travel with other adult students who are learning English as a second language (ESL). The program takes place at a language college in Oxford. The students will live in B+B homes with friendly English speaking hosts. The college is the UWE School, Universal Worldwide Education, for English as a Second Language in Oxford. In weekly lessons, we will improve our English speaking skills from level A2 to B2.

The days will be packed with different activities. In the mornings, you'll have English lessons at UWE-School. The afternoons are free for you to explore Oxford and other parts of England. In the evenings, you can relax with friends at pubs or enjoy cultural events. The main teaching method focuses on learning language patterns using complete sentences. By repeating and practicing these patterns regularly, you will improve your English speaking skills.

In this blog, you can read and hear the weekly reports from the lessons and the everyday activities. You'll have the opportunity to do exercises, complete worksheets and write your own letters or reports. Speaking English fluently is the most important goal of teaching. This trip offers a great way to learn English while experiencing English culture first-hand.

Read the stories and listen to the recordings of Udo's journey.

01 Udo arrives in Oxford

The scent of freshly baked bread, warm and yeasty, hangs in the crisp Oxford air. It mingles with the earthy aroma of damp leaves as you pull your worn leather suitcase behind you. The cobblestone street, lined with houses painted in cheerful pastel shades, feels a world away from the familiar concrete of home.

You pause before number fifteen, the brass number gleaming softly in the afternoon light. This is it: your B&B for the next month. A nervous flutter dances in your stomach, a mix of anticipation and the quiet fear of the unknown. You straighten your jacket, a familiar, comforting motion.

Taking a deep breath, you ascend the three stone steps to the front door. The wood is painted a glossy, inviting blue. You reach for the doorbell, a small, antique-looking contraption, and give it a firm press. A melodic chime echoes from within.

You can hear movement inside, the soft pad of footsteps approaching. The door swings open, revealing a woman with a warm smile and kind eyes. "Udo, welcome! I'm Mrs. Davies. We've been expecting you."

Her voice is gentle, with a lilting accent that's both foreign and strangely comforting. "Come in, come in," she says, gesturing you inside. "You must be tired from your journey."

The hallway is cozy and inviting, a riot of floral wallpaper and antique furniture. A grandfather clock ticks rhythmically in the corner, marking the passage of time with steady, unwavering precision. The air is thick with the scent of potpourri and something else… something savory and comforting.

"Let me take your suitcase," Mrs. Davies offers, her smile unwavering. "Your room is just upstairs. It's number three."

You relinquish your suitcase, the weight lifting from your arm. "Thank you, Mrs. Davies. It's a pleasure to be here."

"The pleasure is all ours, dear," she says, her eyes twinkling. "Now, go on up and get settled. Tea will be ready in about an hour. It's a good way to know the other students."

02 Welcome to the B&B

You mount the narrow staircase, the wooden steps creaking softly under your weight. The scent of lavender grows stronger as you ascend. At the top of the stairs, you find a small landing with three doors. Number three is clearly marked with a small brass plate. You reach for the handle, a sense of quiet anticipation building within you.

As you step into the room, you notice a small, handwritten note lying on the neatly made bed. It's addressed to you. The note is written in elegant cursive. You read: "Welcome, Udo! We're so glad to have you. Tea will be served in the main dining room at 4 PM. Looking forward to meeting you! - Mrs. Davies".

A smile spreads across your face. Tea at four. A perfect English welcome. You set the note aside and begin unpacking. Your clothes, neatly folded, find their place in the small wardrobe. Toiletries are arranged on the shelf in the even smaller en-suite bathroom. Soon, your suitcase is empty, tucked away under the bed.

With time to spare, you decide to freshen up. The water in the shower is hot and plentiful, washing away the weariness of your journey. Refreshed, you dress in your best casual attire. You want to make a good first impression.

As you descend the stairs, you hear the murmur of voices from the dining room. It seems you are not the first to arrive. Taking a deep breath, you step into the room. The dining room is bright and cheerful, bathed in the warm glow of the late afternoon sun. A long, polished table dominates the room, laden with an array of cakes, sandwiches, and teapots. Several people are already seated, chatting animatedly.

03 Smalltalk in the evening

Mrs. Davies bustles about, pouring tea and offering plates of food. "Ah, Udo, you're here! Come, come, let me introduce you." She leads you to the table. "Everyone, this is Udo, our new student from Germany. Udo, this is Maria from Spain, Kenji from Japan, and Aaliyah from Saudi Arabia."

Maria, a young woman with bright eyes and a warm smile, greets you with enthusiasm. "Welcome to Oxford, Udo! I hope you enjoy your time here." Kenji, a quiet and polite young man, nods his head in greeting. "It's nice to meet you." Aaliyah, a confident and articulate woman, extends her hand. "Welcome! I am sure you will fit in quickly."

"Please, everyone, make Udo feel at home," Mrs. Davies says, beaming. "Now, help yourselves to tea and sandwiches. And don't be shy, practice your English!" You take a seat at the table, feeling a surge of anticipation. "Thank you. It's a pleasure to meet you all." You pour yourself a cup of tea, the fragrant steam rising to your face.

"So, Udo, what brings you to Oxford?" Maria asks, her eyes sparkling with curiosity. Before you can answer, a booming voice fills the room. "Sorry I'm late! Traffic was a nightmare."

A tall man with a neatly trimmed beard strides into the dining room. "Mrs. Davies, my apologies. Ah, we have a new face! Welcome to UWE School." Mrs. Davies turns to you with a smile: "Udo, this is Tom, head of the teaching staff."
